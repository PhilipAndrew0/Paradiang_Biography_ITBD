"Chasing Dreams: The Story of my Self-Discovery from Childhood to College"

CHILDHOOD STORY

     1My name is Philip Andrew Paradiang. 2 I grew up in Tondo, Manila, and this is where I was born too. 3 My parents were always supportive, encouraging me to be curious and explore the world around me. 4 I always wanted to play outside, and sometimes I wanted to skip school just to play, but my parents wouldn’t let me. 5 When it was my first time going to school, I cried because I didn’t want my mom to leave me there. 6 My mom would read bedtime stories to me and my siblings. 7 Even though I was still young, I liked to spend time drawing, making little sketches of the things I saw in anime. 8 I wasn’t great at art, but it felt fun and relaxing. 9 School was a place where I could make friends and show what I had learned. 10 I’m not a smart kid, but my teachers were very kind and helped me grow. 11 I love visiting my grandparents because they’re so kind to me, and I love them. 12 I also spent a lot of time with my friends from the neighborhood. 13 We would ride our bikes around the block or play hide and seek outside. 14 Our days were filled with simple games like luksong baka, hide and seek, and tumbang preso. 15 On rainy days, I was happy when the class got suspended and I got to watch cartoons all day. 16 The town I grew up in was lively, and I still cherish the friendships I made here. 17 My childhood was a time of exploration, learning, and laying the foundation for my future. 18 My parents always found a way to give us opportunities to learn and grow. 19 Those moments made me feel happy and safe, and they helped me grow into who I am today. 20 Looking back, I realize how much those small moments shaped my love for nature, learning, and being creative.

TEENAGE STORY

school, I learned that there was more to life besides playing. 3 I discovered new hobbies like playing online games and basketball. 4 I’m not that good at those things, but if they make you happy, you don’t have to listen to what other people say. 5 When I became a teenager, everything started to change. 6 High school was both exciting and nerve-wracking at the same time. 7 I had to adjust to new classes, teachers, and a larger group of students. 8 At first, I felt a little lost and unsure of myself, but I soon made new friends who shared similar interests, i gained self-confidence. 9 I was a problem child when I was in high school, so my parents got called often by my teachers because I was so naughty. 10 But even if I was like this, I still prioritized my grades to not let my parents COLLEGE STORY

     1College was a big step forward for me. 2 I chose this school (University of the East) because this is where my brother graduated, and the IT program here is a Center of Excellence. 3 At first, college felt overwhelming because everything was new, but I managed to meet new friends on our first day. 4 I admit that college is very hard, and for me, this is survival, and you have to take this seriously. 5 I can’t catch up to other lessons no matter how hard I try because I’m a slow learner, but I’m doing my best to reach my dreams and make my parents proud. 6 At first, I felt pressured by the amount of work and the level of difficulty in my classes. 7 There were times when I doubted myself, thinking I wasn’t good enough to succeed. 8 But I kept pushing myself to study, ask questions, and get help when I needed it. 9 I’ve learned to manage my time and submit my assignments on time, even when I feel like it’s too much. 10 I also have reliable friends whom I can ask if there’s something I don’t understand. 11 I’m grateful to have them as my friends, and these are some things that money can’t buy. 12 Being a college student taught me that making mistakes is fine as long as you learn from them. 13 This level is also a learning opportunity for me to help me grow more, reach my dreams, and also become financially stable. 14 College has definitely been a journey of growth for me. 15 There have been days when I felt like giving up, but I reminded myself that I’m here to learn, not to be perfect. 16 I’ve come to realize that everyone struggles with something, and it’s okay to not have all the answers right away. 17 Being a college student isn’t just about being smart but about being responsible and willing to keep trying. 18 It’s also about finding balance and taking care of myself, even when school gets stressful. 19 College has helped me grow in ways I never expected, and I’m learning to believe in myself more. 20 I’m not perfect, but I’m doing my best, and that’s what really matters.
